Stingray

Stingrays get their name from the stinging barb at the base of their tail. When they feel threatened Stingrays can whip up their tail to puncture their pursuer with their spiny, venomous barb. They also like to bury themselves in the sand when they have to hide from their own predators. The cost of this attraction is going to be $20 per person.
