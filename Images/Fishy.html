Recent Facts

Clownfish

Did you know that clownfish live in anemones that act as a natural barrier to predators due to their tenticles that release toxins. This in turn means that the clownfish have to slowly build up a resistance to this toxin by making a layer of mucus build up through carfully touching the tenticles.

Shark

Did you know that sharks can go into a trance when they are flipped upside down. This is due to tonic immobility which is also known as animal hypnosis. It is a form of natural paralysis which scientist use to study a shark. It is caused by a shark being flipped on it's back

Dolphin

Did you know that dolphins make sounds so that they can communicate. They can communicate to other dolphins by whistling. Scientist even predict that this is also how they communicate with other animals.

Hot Facts

Stingray

Did you know that stingrays are a very old species that have existed for over 100 million years. It has been found that there are even fossil records that date them back as far as 150 years.

Penguin

Did you know that not all penguins live in Antartica. Some species live in South Africa, Chile and Peru to name a few. This means that most are found in the southern hemisphere except the galapagos penguin that can be found living on the equator.

Sea Lion

Did you know that sea lions usually swim at about 11 miles an hour. The surprising thing is that they can double that speed and go up to 25 miles an hour
